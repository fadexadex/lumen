## Design Context

### Users

Lumen is for school-age learners, especially curious teenagers, working through mathematics with a real-time voice tutor. The learner needs to understand an idea, see it represented spatially, work examples on an open board, interrupt naturally, and return without losing context.

### Brand Personality

Calm, intelligent, and encouraging. Lumen should feel like a patient tutor sharing one quiet workspace with the learner: concise without being abrupt, polished without feeling corporate, and visually expressive without becoming distracting.

### Aesthetic Direction

Use Lumen's existing light, editorial whiteboard language: warm near-white surfaces, charcoal ink, restrained amber accents, Instrument Serif for display and mathematical voice, and Inter for prose and controls. Prefer open space, fine rules, hand-drawn annotations, and subtle material depth. Motion should clarify mathematical change or sequence; avoid ornamental dashboards, heavy gradients, dense cards, cartoon classroom decoration, and unrelated graphics. Dark mode is not currently a product requirement.

### Design Principles

1. Keep one visual authority: lesson content, generated visuals, camera focus, annotations, and Live tutor context must refer to the same typed board objects.
2. Show the mathematical relationship, not decoration. Select a visual because it improves understanding and allow an explicit `none` when it does not.
3. Preserve a stable reading frame. New content should appear in predictable board regions and the camera should move only when the active idea would otherwise be out of view.
4. Animate meaning. Use short, interruptible, deterministic transitions for transformations, grouping, balance, plotting, and step reveals; respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
5. Keep generated UI safe and consistent. Models choose and parameterize trusted React/SVG primitives; they never author HTML, CSS, JavaScript, or arbitrary coordinates.
6. Target WCAG 2.2 AA by default, including keyboard-operable controls, color-independent labels, readable contrast, responsive layout, and reduced-motion behavior.
