## Design Context

### Users
Teenagers studying alone. They want the feeling of a real tutor writing with them on the board — guided, patient, not lecture-y. Sessions happen in quiet focus time (bedroom desk, library, after school), often on laptop or tablet.

### Brand Personality
Calm · clear · encouraging. The interface should feel like a place where learning is easy to stay in: low pressure, high clarity, gentle progress. Confidence without cheerleading.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light paper / live tutor whiteboard. Warm neutrals with a soft amber accent. Instrument Serif for display titles and equations; Inter for teaching prose; handwriting (Caveat) only for ink notes and chalk-style concepts — not the default lesson voice. Prefer quiet textbook-desk clarity over costume “handwritten everything.” Anti-patterns: loud floating banners, stacked chrome colliding on the right edge, cream equation cards, purple-glow AI aesthetics, dark neon tutor UIs.

### Design Principles
1. **Board first** — lesson content owns the viewport; chrome stays secondary and easy to reach.
2. **Tutor presence** — typing, spacing, and math should feel written *with* the student, not stamped on top.
3. **Calm density** — tight groupings for related beats, generous air between sections; never cramped chrome.
4. **One primary action** — Play / Continue lives in the control rail; no competing banners.
5. **Readable math** — equations stay crisp and serif; prose stays calm sans; ink is the only place for hand.
